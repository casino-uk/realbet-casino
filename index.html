How to Verify a Casino Licence Yourself

  1. Open the UKGC public register of businesses (gamblingcommission.gov.uk).
  2. Search for the operator's company name, not just the brand — a casino's footer should name the licensed company.
  3. Check the licence is active, covers remote casino, and lists the website's domain as a trading name.
  4. If the brand or company isn't there, it is not UKGC-licensed — regardless of any badge or text on the site.
  5. Be aware: it is illegal to offer gambling to people in Great Britain without a UKGC licence, and you get no UK protections on unlicensed sites.

What a Real UKGC Licence Gives You

UKGC-licensed

Segregated player funds, independently tested RNGs, GamStop self-exclusion, mandatory deposit/session limits, and complaints escalation to a UKGC-approved ADR.

Offshore-only / unlicensed

No UK deposit protection, no GamStop, no UKGC ADR. Dispute options are limited and enforcement leverage is weak. Withdrawal problems can be hard to resolve.

A site can print "UKGC licensed" or show a logo without being licensed. Only the public register is authoritative.

Common Red Flags

  • "UKGC licensed" with no company name or licence number to check
  • Regulator logos shown but no entry on the public register
  • Vague or shifting terms on bonuses and withdrawals
  • Pressure to deposit quickly to "unlock" a bonus
  • Reports of stalled or refused withdrawals in independent reviews

If You Choose to Play Anyway

  • Deposit only what you can afford to lose
